Three findings worth reading first

What this work surfaced

Governance is only as good as what it actually catches. These are the three insights an evaluator should take away — lifted directly from the dossier’s executive summary.

1 Vendor asymmetry

The auxiliary data vendor carried more risk than the foundation model

The model provider held SOC 2, ISO 27001, and ISO/IEC 42001 with a published DPA. The job-data aggregator published none of these — and the free tier carried no liability shield. The most-overlooked risk sat in the least-visible component.

2 Defense in depth

Prevention outperformed after-the-fact scrubbing

A high-severity PII leak was met with two parallel controls. Re-testing showed the prompt-template constraint fired first — leaving the scrubbing layer with nothing to redact. The desirable outcome, but it created a new monitoring obligation, documented as an open item.

3 Closing the loop

Findings without close-out are just inventory

The full lifecycle — protocol, execution, remediation, re-execution, close-out — ran against a real codebase in real time. Five of six findings closed; one was deliberately retained as accepted residual risk, with the reasoning written down.

Risk classification

A four-stage decision cascade

Rather than a scoring sheet that invites endless debate, the framework asks a short sequence of yes/no questions. AGT-001 lands at T2 — Limited, driven by a single elevated factor: conditional autonomy.

The red-team story

Find it, fix it, prove it stayed fixed

The operational core. A 15-case protocol aligned to NIST AI 600-1 was executed against the real agent, remediated at the code level, and re-executed thirty minutes later — producing a fail-to-pass evidence trail.

Cycle 1 — initial
9 / 15
cases passed. Six findings — including a high-severity structured PII exfiltration via schema-extension prompt injection.
Remediation
4 code-level fixes: schema enforcement, PII redaction, prompt-template hardening, audit logging
Cycle 2 — post-fix
14 / 15
cases passed. Five findings closed; one retained as accepted residual risk. Zero regressions.
“Where multiple defenses target the same risk surface, the prompt-template constraint fires first — leaving the post-processing scrubbing layer with nothing to redact.”
The desirable outcome — but it means the scrubbing layer is rarely exercised in production, so it must be independently tested each quarter. A fix that created a new monitoring obligation, documented rather than hidden.
